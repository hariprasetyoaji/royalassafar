There’s no question that one of Japan’s biggest attractions for many visitors is the food, and more specifically, the sushi.

Kanazawa’s Kitchen

With its proximity to the sea and surrounded by fertile farmland, Kanazawa holds a reputation throughout the country as a gourmet paradise, luring even sophisticated Tokyo diners for weekend epicurean escapes. One of the best places to see everything the region has to offer is Omicho Ichiba, the city’s sprawling covered market.

Take a tour 

Of everything on offer at Omicho, nothing draws serious shoppers more than the seafood. With the Sea of Japan less than 10 miles to the west, supporting Kanazawa’s thriving fishing industry, everything from tiny hotaru ika (“firefly” squid) to enormous magaro is colorfully arrayed in stall after stall. As most of the hand-scrawled signs identifying the wares are written in Japanese, it’s helpful to have a knowledgeable guide along to decipher and explain what’s what.

A lesson with a master chef

Go one step further, and see it in the company of local experts, including the master chef at one of Kanazawa’s premier sushi restaurants. After the tour, he’ll whisk you up to a private classroom for an interactive lesson in sushi preparation, culminating in a delicious and impossibly fresh lunch.
